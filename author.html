The Series: Bridgerton

Synopsis

Bridgerton is a TV series on Netflix
The Bridgerton family are entirely fictional and were not a real family in the Regency era.
The series is based on a fictional set of novels written by Julia Quinn.
Bridgerton follows Daphne Bridgerton, the eldest daughter of the powerful Bridgerton family as she makes her debut onto Regency London's competitive marriage market. Hoping to follow in her parents' footsteps and find a match sparked by true love, Daphne's prospects initially seem to be unrivaled.

Jane Austen. Gossip Girl. Shonda Rhimes. Diverse casts. If one or more of these things speak to you, you're probably hooked on Netflix's latest marathon-able show.
The new series Bridgerton follows the well-to-do Bridgerton family, a widow and her eight, alphabetically named children, as they navigate Regency era English society. And guess what?
Regency era English society is full of gossip and secrets.
There’s a fake relationship, a surprise pregnancy, a dramatic deathbed vow, an affair with an opera singer, an actual duel, and a sassy narrator voiced by Dame Julie Andrews.
And that’s all in the first few episodes.
